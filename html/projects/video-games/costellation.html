OVERVIEW

Take a little break from your hectic day to gaze at the stars!

Costellation is the result of a 3 and a half day workshop with the objective to create an interactive experience with basic inputs (keyboard, mouse, joystick) but outputs would not be limited to a computer screen.

My duties included:

  • Develop gameplay features (sky generation, constellation creation…)
  • Asset integration (2D sprites, animations…)
  • Performance control and optimization
  • Collaborating closely with environment artists, sound designers and game designers
  • Maintain a feature list with regular updates so that everyone can track progress
  • And more!

STRUCTURE

Using a projector, a mirror, leds and scraps, we built a tent that immerses the player in the atmosphere of an evening watching the starry sky.

The experience isn't limited to observation: the player also has a joystick that they can use to draw their own constellations! Placed in the tent, a notebook allows players to draw and name their creation, so that future players can find it.

GAMEPLAY

To create a constellation, the player uses a Xbox controller. They can move around with the left stick, create a new constellation segment and cancel their last segment. Each time someone creates a new constellation, its opacity diminishes so that it can be seen but does not disturb the next player when they create their own.

The cursor is programmed to have free movements but snaps to the closest star to make it easier for players to select their next star.

We used LineRenderers for the constellation segments, as well as to preview a segment before creating it with a specific material, width, and color.

Pressing A and finishing a constellation sends a message to an Arduino board that lights up a few LEDs hooked above the tent, for a nice visual feedback.
