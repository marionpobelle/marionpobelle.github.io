About

Hello!

I’m a game programmer who recently graduated from ENJMIN with a Master in Game Programming. Over the past few years, I’ve been able to work on a wide variety of games with different types, platforms and technologies.

My experience as a game developer started with the Develop At Ubisoft 2023 program, a mentorship during which I was guided by a senior gameplay programmer at Ubisoft Bordeaux. It was followed by an internship opportunity to participate in a research program where I implemented a game prototype for the National Museum of Natural History of Paris. Finally at the end of my Master's, I joined Toe Bean Club as an intern gameplay programmer, and I am fortunate to be working with them as a freelance game programmer now.

I’ve worked with several different teams, made many friends, and learned several different game production processes along the way, but what makes me unique is that I enjoy all of the different programming specializations and that I am not afraid to learn.

Why is this important?

As a generalist, I’m quick to learn, adapt, and leverage all of that experience to mold whatever we are making into something solid, juicy and fun. I love to try and see how things can be put together in an efficient and scalable way, and often ask my peers for feedback to ensure my plans for implementing features fit their design.

This desire to connect makes me a better teammate and mentor. Collaborating with others motivates me because I enjoy working towards a collective goal with people. I have also mentored other team members and helped them discover programming. I strive to be empathetic and serve my peers to help us all achieve greatness and have fun while doing it.

I love learning new things, growing, and pouring my passion into making games. I hope to keep doing it for many years to come.

Outside of game development, I love baking and cooking, tea, cats, books and comics, collecting DVDs, longboarding, board games, watching movies, anime and series, and of course, video games!
